Cooking:

The classic Italian drink is prepared according to
the following recipe: one shot of espresso is poured
into a ceramic cup from a coffee machine, after
which heated and steamed milk is poured into the
cup. Sometimes, when pouring milk into espresso,
baristas use special movements to create a pattern
on the surface of the drink - this technique is called
latte art. Espresso quality, milk temperature, consis
tency and proportions are very important to obtain
, cappuccino is served in a preheated ceramic cup
with a volume of no more than 210 ml[2]. In Italy,
cappuccino is served in a special cup - tazza -
made of thick- walled ceramic. As with espresso,
a cappuccino cup must be preheated, so in coffee
shops these cups are stored on coffee machines,
heated by heat from boilers[4].
